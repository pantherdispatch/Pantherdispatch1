Everything You Need to Know About IFTA Report

By: Jaguar Dispatch Services fab 23, 2023

What is an IFTA Report?

The International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) report is a treaty between the lower 48 states of the United States and ten provinces of Canada. This agreement simplifies the reporting of fuel consumption by interstate motor carriers operating in the United States and Canada. The purpose of the IFTA is to ensure that each state or province gets its fair share of fuel taxes from motor carriers. IFTA doesn't include Hawaii, Alaska, and the District of Columbia.

The leading cause behind the IFTA report is to replace the old and outdated fuel tax system. In the past, trucks were mandated to have separate decals for each state they operated in. The new IFTA reporting system makes fuel tax reporting hassle-free for truck operators by lessening the paperwork and minimizing compliance requirements.

IFTA is not an additional tax to overburden the drivers. IFTA was essentially made to redistribute the tax to states where fuel is actually used rather than where it is purchased. Meaning that regardless of where you buy fuel, you are paying fuel tax in all states in which you drive.

Who Must Get an IFTA License?

Not everyone needs to get the IFTA license. However, you're required to get one if you fall under one of the following categories:

  • Your vehicle travels in one or more dominions
  • Your car is used for commercial purposes
  • Your vehicle has two axles and a registered weight of over 26,000 alone or when combined with a trailer.
How to Calculate IFTA Fuel Tax Reports?

IFTA costs vary depending on your vehicle weight, driving location, and the state you purchase fuel. Below are some easy steps that can help you calculate the IFTA fuel tax.

Track the miles you've traveled in each state

In order to accurately record the amount of fuel consumed in various jurisdictions, fleet managers and drivers must collaborate. This demands the utmost organization for fleets when managing their driverâ€™s duty status records.

Every time they cross state lines, drivers must diligently record their odometer readings to be more precise.

Add fuel purchases

The next thing you want to do for your IFTA fuel tax report is the information on the total gallons of fuel purchased in each state. Original receipts and invoices must be kept to prove fuel tax was paid.

Calculate the fuel consumed in each state

Using the miles traveled and fuel purchase data, you will need to calculate the fuel consumed by each vehicle in each jurisdiction during the reporting period. This can be done using the fuel economy data for each vehicle.

Calculator taxable fuel

Once you have calculated the fuel consumed, you can then calculate the taxable fuel for each jurisdiction by multiplying the fuel consumed by the tax rate for that jurisdiction.

Calculate due tax by putting it all together

Due tax can be easily calculated by multiplying each jurisdiction's taxable fuel by the tax rate of that particular jurisdiction.

When is IFTA Report Required to be Filed?

The IFTA reporting is due every three months or every quarter. You should document your report by the last day of the month following the end of the quarter. Here are the particular filing cutoff times for each quarter:

  • The due date from January through March is the 30th of April
  • The due date from April through June is the 31st of July
  • The due date from July through September is the 31st of October
  • The due date from October through December is the 31st of January
How to Report IFTA Fuel Tax?

There are various ways of filing an IFTA report, and you can choose the one that suits you best:

  • Online: The filing is considered received the day you submit it.
  • Mail: The filing is considered received by the postmark date.
  • In-person: The filing is considered received by the day it is delivered to the office.
What Happens if You Don't File Your IFTA Report on Time?

Submitting your IFTA report on time is essential to maintain legal compliance and avoid penalties. If you fail to file your IFTA report on time, you may face several consequences.

Late filing penalties

If you don't file the IFTA report on time, states and provinces may impose penalties on you. The penalties can be a percentage of the due tax or a fixed fleet per day.

Interest charges

You could also be charged interest if you don't pay the tax by the deadline. These charges accumulate on the unpaid tax balance, which can quickly increase the amount owed.

Suspension of license

Your IFTA license may be suspended if you have a history of late or non-filing IFTA reports. This means that you won't be able to drive your commercial vehicles until you have filed all of the reports that need to be filed and paid the penalties and interest that go along with them.

Audits and investigations

Audits and investigations may also be more likely if IFTA reports are not submitted on time. This can be time-consuming and costly, as auditors will review all records and transactions to ensure compliance with IFTA regulations.

It is essential to stay organized and up to date and keep track of IFTA deadlines, as failing to file the resorts on time might result in financial and operational consequences.

How Does IFTA Help You Save More Money?

The International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) is designed to help you save money in several ways. Because of the standardized reporting system by IFTA, the process of reporting fuel tax is simplified. This helps in reducing time and cost, and fleet managers do not have to file separate resorts for each state or province.

IFTA reporting also saves the time and effort required for manual data entry, as all the filing can be done electronically. Fleet managers can avoid overpaying taxes as IFTA allows them to claim credits for fuel taxes paid in other jurisdictions. This maximizes savings and lowers the amount of tax owed.

Moreover, you can also save money on fuel by tracking fuel consumption and mileage. This helps in identifying the areas of inefficiency and making adjustments to reduce fuel consumption. IFTA also provides detailed and accurate data to help drivers and fleet managers make well-informed decisions regarding route planning, vehicle maintenance, and other cost-saving measures.

Conclusion

Understanding the International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) report is essential for fleet managers who operate commercial vehicles in multiple jurisdictions. Fleet managers can stay in compliance with the law and avoid penalties by adhering to the reporting requirements and submitting IFTA reports on time. Additionally, fleet managers can save time and money by using IFTA software or seeking assistance from a tax professional to simplify the reporting procedure.
