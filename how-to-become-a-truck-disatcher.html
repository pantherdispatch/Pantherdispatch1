How to Become a Truck Dispatcher | A Definitive Guide

Being a truck dispatcher comes with a lot of perks, the main being job stability. The trucking industry is a crucial part of the economy and is expected to grow in the coming years, creating more demand for professional truck dispatchers. However, being a truck dispatcher is not always fun and games, especially when you're just starting. In this blog, you'll learn everything you need to become a successful truck dispatcher, both as an employee and independently.

What Are the Duties of a Truck Dispatcher?

Truck dispatchers coordinate and direct truck drivers' schedules to ensure that the items and goods are dispatched and shipped promptly. They answer calls for truck services and then contact their appropriate truck drivers to manage the pickup and deliveries. They are constantly in contact with the drivers throughout their journey to monitor their progress and help them with any issues that might occur throughout the route.

Truck dispatchers are also responsible for passing on information to customers to keep them informed of the estimated delivery dates. A truck dispatcher is usually confused with a freight broker; however, both have entirely distinct roles. A freight broker is a person who is the middleman between the person who wants to ship the cargo (a shipper) and the person who ships the load, i.e., the carrier. A freight broker can represent both parties simultaneously without any personal bias to any side.

On the other hand, a truck dispatcher works for a carrier. Even if you work as an independent freight dispatcher, you are still, in essence, an employee of the carrier you currently work for. Whenever you negotiate with a freight broker, you do so on the carrier's behalf. In contrast to brokers, truck dispatchers are not permitted by law to represent shippers or manufacturers.

Things You Should Know if You Want to Become a Truck Dispatcher

Is there any qualification needed to become a truck dispatcher?

Although some employers require additional qualifications, most truck dispatcher jobs don't demand anything other than a high school diploma. However, getting a qualification like an associate degree significantly boosts your chances of getting the job. If you want to improve your understanding and knowledge of the subject, you could also enroll in a specialized truck dispatcher course. However, to be considered valid, you must ensure that the course is taken at an accredited institution.

How long does it take to become a truck dispatcher?

The time it takes to become a truck dispatcher can vary depending on several factors, including your education, experience, and job requirements.

Becoming a truck dispatcher can typically take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months. Some employers may provide on-the-job training that can last several weeks or months. In contrast, others may require more formal education, such as completing a certificate or diploma program.

If you choose to pursue a formal education, certificate, or diploma program in truck dispatching, it may take anywhere from several weeks to a year to complete, depending on the school and program structure.

It's important to note that experience in the transportation industry, such as working as a driver or in a logistics role, can also be valuable when pursuing a career as a truck dispatcher. In short, the amount of time it takes to become a truck dispatcher will depend on your circumstances and career goals.

Essential skills to become a truck dispatcher

To become a successful truck dispatcher, you must possess the following skills:

  • Excellent communication skills
  • Strong organizational skills
  • Attention to detail
  • Technology expertise
  • Critical thinking skills
  • Ability to work under pressure
  • Customer service skills

How to Become a Dispatcher for a Trucking Company?

If you're thinking of starting your career as a truck dispatcher and you don't know where to start, the following are the easiest steps you can follow to begin your dispatching career:

Have compulsory education and training

Contrary to the other careers in the trucking industry, a truck dispatcher doesn't need to have higher education. In order to get the job, you don't need to have a college or university degree. However, most employers won't consider you for the role if you don't have a high school diploma or GED. It is also recommended to enroll in a dispatch course to polish your skills and add credibility to your job application.

If possible, get an associate degree

Track dispatching deals with a number of disciplines, including transportation, logistics, and shipping. An associate's degree in one of these related fields will help you stand out from other applicants. Community colleges and technical schools offer these degrees in just two years, often with schedules that work for students with other professional or personal commitments. An associate's degree can also put you on track to earn a bachelor's degree halfway, depending on your level of interest. However, this is not compulsory, and higher education is not required to become a truck dispatcher.

Gain truck dispatching experience

Having industry experience will surely make you stand out from the other candidates. Usually, a truck dispatcher's work is not limited to the office, but having relevant experience can turn out to be a bonus point for you. You might want to work in freight hauling or shipping roles. As truck dispatching is connected to many other disciplines, having an understanding of other people's roles can also give you an edge.

Build and upgrade your skills

The two most crucial skill sets a truck dispatcher needs are exceptional communication and exemplary organizational skills. Being a truck dispatcher demands paying close attention to the details and communicating consistently. They must build and retain close relationships with carriers, brokers, and shippers. It is critical to make relations and handle customer requirements. This requires networking, problem-solving, and handling stressful situations effectively.

It is also essential for a truck dispatcher to have a command of handling requests and queries and communicate over the phone, email, and in person. Time management is also a significant part of the role and is vital to becoming a successful truck dispatcher.

Get ready and then apply for the job

One way to ensure that you're ready to apply for the jobs and have competitive skills is to opt for informational interviews. These interviews surely enhance your knowledge and industry-related information and are a great way to make you more confident in your skill set.

Once you're ready, find the job that suits you! Online searches, job boards, and even the relationships you build in the industry can help you find the perfect job for you.

How to Become an Independent Truck Dispatcher?

If you prefer to avoid working under other companies or want to establish your own business, you should have all the necessary skills and education to become a dispatcher. After you've got those skills and are ready to take the venture, below are the steps you can take to start your business:

Register your business

The first and foremost step of starting as an independent truck dispatcher is naming your business and registering it. When deciding on a name for your company, try to keep it simple and relevant to help people get an idea of what your business is about. When you've chosen the name for your company, the next step is to apply for the Employer Identification Number with the IRS and decide on your business structure. It is best to apply for Inc. or LLC structure.

Create a robust online presence

A solid online presence and effective marketing strategy require creating a user-friendly website and creating accounts on various social media channels. Select the website name that includes your company's name and keep it short and straightforward. Get your desired domain name and then register it.

Once you've your website, create accounts on Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and even YouTube with the name, as it will empower you to reach a larger audience.

Subscribe to a reliable load board

Finding relevant, high-quality loads for your carriers is an essential part of being a truck dispatcher. To accomplish this, you will need to subscribe to a reputable load board, which will provide you with access to thousands of nationwide freight listings. While there are free load boards, if you want to find high-quality freight for your carriers, you need to spend money on a paid subscription board.

With thousands of freight listings every single day, Jaguar Dispatch Services load board is the ultimate choice for anyone starting their business as a truck dispatcher.

Make and maintain connections

One of the crucial factors determining the success of your business is how you deal with people. As soon as you step into the industry, try to make connections with brokers and shippers. Always be open to communication and provide satisfactory answers to any queries or doubts.
