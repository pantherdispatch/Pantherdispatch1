What is The Key Difference Between MC Number and DOT Number

By: Jaguar Dispatch Services March 2, 2023

What is The Key Difference Between MC Number and DOT Number

If you are in the trucking business, you must have heard about the MC number and DOT number. These numbers are vital for commercial carriers and are issued by the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) in the United States. The MC number and DOT number serve different purposes and have distinct requirements.

In this article, we will explain the key differences between these two numbers and their importance for commercial carriers.

What is MC Number and Why is it Important?

The MC number is also called the "MX" or "FF" number, which can be obtained from an FMCSA operating authority. This number gives the operator an operating authority that is validated by Federal law in the U.S.

MC truck number is a unique number assigned by the FMCSA to create a unique identifier that allows FMCSA to track records, security, and compliance easily. If a company transports people for compensation either directly or indirectly across state lines, it must obtain an MC number.

Moreover, businesses that transport regulated goods in a similar manner for the purpose of commerce or compensation are required to provide this number. Additionally, if a carrier operates in a commercial zone as defined by federal law, they must obtain an MC number.

What is a DOT Number, and Why is it Important?

A USDOT number is a unique identifier and an operating authority that the FMCSA assigns to the transport company that performs interstate operations. This number makes it easier for authorities to keep track of the company's safety records, compliance, reviews, crash investigations, and more.

The purpose of the creation of the DOT number was to reduce the number of accidents that cause property damage or life loss due to improperly trained drivers and the vehicle companies not ensuring compliance. All this is done by tracking the accidents and incidents. If a company is considered unsafe, there are high chances of certificate cancellation.

The Key Differences Between MC Number and DOT Number

Although both MC and DOT numbers are unique identifiers for commercial carriers, a few things set them apart.

  • Safety and maintenance are the prime purposes of the DOT number. On the other hand, the MC number is used for more than just tracking carriers across state lines.
  • Likewise, getting an MC number is commonly more complicated than getting a DOT number. A thorough application and supporting documents must be submitted to the FMCSA office in your area.
  • Finally, the requirements for obtaining an MC number typically exceed those for getting a DOT number. You might have to have specific insurance, for instance. You may also be required to meet specific operational standards in order to qualify for the MC number.
  • While all interstate movers are obligated to have and display a DOT number on their commercial carriers, not all movers are required to have an MC number.
Who Needs a DOT Number?

Any transportation company that moves vehicles across state lines must have a USDOT number. The number is needed for both types of companies, cargo hauling and passenger transporting. It is compulsory for each moving company to get registered with FMCSA.

Who Needs an MC Number?

Unlike the DOT number, not all companies that carry out interstate operations need an MC number. The following businesses generally need to obtain the MC number:

  • For hire companies
  • Companies transporting people or goods for reimbursement
  • Interstate commerce transportation companies
  • Companies transporting people or goods, whatever the compensation

How to Get DOT and MC Number?

How to acquire a DOT number?

The DOT number is assigned by the FMCSA, the authority that monitors all commercial vehicles in the United States. If you want to get a DOT number, you need to apply to your local FMCSA office to do so. Here are the requirements for getting a USDOT number:

  • Information about your business (name, location, etc.)
  • Your personal information
  • Information about the operation classification you are looking to get
  • Know whether you will move cargo or people
  • A valid address
  • Your business insurance policy
  • A BOC-3 form

If you have all this information, apply for the USDOT number right away at your local FMCSA office. The number is usually given right away if there isn't any issue with the application. However, you'll have to wait for 25 days to get operating authority.

How to acquire an MC number?

To obtain an MC number, you must first apply and satisfy specific eligibility criteria. This generally includes providing details about your business and its safety record. It also involves passing a written test and paying any applicable fees.

Depending on where you live, the specific application process will be different. Therefore consult the transport safety authority or your local FMCSA office for proper guidance.

Understanding the Importance of MC Number and DOT Number for Your Business

MC and DOT numbers are crucial for any commercial operators in the U.S. These numbers serve as a unique identifier and help track and check the vehicle for compliance and safety records.

There are many factors involved in the decision to get an MC and DOT number. Understanding the difference between the two and the requirements of both is essential for the compliance and success of your business.
