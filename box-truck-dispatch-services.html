What are Box Truck Dispatch Services?

Characterized by its “box-like” shape, the box truck is a commercial loading vehicle in which the cargo area and the cab are separated. The cargo is shaped like a box, giving the truck its unique name. Box truck is also referred to as straight truck or cube truck. Most box truck services allow a hauling capacity of 7,400 to 10,000 lbs.

Box trucks essentially work as smaller dry vans and are typically used to carry non-perishable items like furniture, electronics, non-perishable food, and textile. Modern box trucks also provide components separated by a door.

Advantages of Box Truck Dispatch Services

No trailer required

One of the peculiar advantages of using a box truck is that it requires no trailer. This saves a lot of time and money for both the box truck dispatcher and the client, as no time is wasted on hauling and mounting a trailer.

Efficiency

Box truck is considered one of the most efficient and expeditious vehicles for partial and less than truckload freight. Most less-than-truckload services don’t work in a timely manner, causing problems for the shipper. These issues can be eliminated by using a box truck, as it ensures on-time deliveries.

Affordability

Box truck is the most cost-effective option out there when it comes to moving your freight, especially in congested areas. Box trucks provide better mileage than larger trucks, making them cost less than heavy loaders.

Safety

Your equipment is less likely to get damaged or stolen when it is contained in a straight truck. The structure of a box truck makes it really hard for thugs to break and steal your stuff. Moreover, the freight is protected from environmental damage and harsh weather.

Box trucks are an essential part of many supply chains and are extremely advantageous in terms of cost and convenience. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) About Box Truck

How much weight can a 16-ft truck carry?

The average 16-ft box truck can haul up to 3,500 lbs. of cargo but the model of the truck heavily influences its hauling capacity.

How many pallets can a straight truck carry?

An average box truck is able to carry 560 pallets at a time. However, the number of pallets a truck carries depends on the model and size of the truck.

What is the best flooring for box trucks?

Mixed hardwood flooring is the most commonly used in box trucks as it is a cost-effective alternative to oak flooring while providing similar benefits.

Can box trucks be customized?

Yes, box trucks are typically customized by manufacturers to meet the specific needs of a business. Box trucks can also be conveniently converted into medical labs, surveillance vans, and more, depending on your requirements.
